Reader-safe sources and starting points for the blog's recurring themes.

These are not homework assignments. They are public doors into the ideas behind
the essays.

## Understanding Stress And Access

- [Stress signalling and prefrontal function](https://doi.org/10.1038/nrn2648),
  Amy Arnsten. A research anchor for why pressure can narrow working memory,
  flexible thinking, and reflective control.
- [Stress, adaptation, and brain plasticity](https://doi.org/10.1152/physrev.00041.2006),
  Bruce McEwen. A source for thinking about repeated stress, allostasis, and how
  experience can reshape future responses.

## Context, Personality, And Patterns

- [A Cognitive-Affective System Theory of Personality](https://doi.org/10.1037/0033-295X.102.2.246),
  Walter Mischel and Yuichi Shoda. A foundation for understanding stable
  "if-then" patterns across different situations.
- [The Ecology of Human Development](https://www.hup.harvard.edu/books/9780674224575),
  Urie Bronfenbrenner. A clear reminder that people develop inside families,
  institutions, cultures, and historical worlds.

## Constraint, Scarcity, And Choice

- [Scarcity](https://us.macmillan.com/books/9781250056115/scarcity/),
  Sendhil Mullainathan and Eldar Shafir. A reader-friendly source for how money,
  time, and other limits can tax attention and narrow choices.

## Resilience And Protective Factors

- [Ordinary Magic](https://doi.org/10.1037/0003-066X.56.3.227), Ann Masten. A
  grounding source for resilience as ordinary protective systems, not a rare
  heroic trait.

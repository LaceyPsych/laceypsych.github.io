# Content Roadmap

## First 12 Longform Posts

1. **Why That Email Felt Like a Trap**
   - Scene: a neutral manager email becomes a full-body threat.
   - Concept: appraisal, context activation, threat vs challenge.
   - Sources: `src-lazarus-folkman-1984-coping`, `src-scherer-2001-appraisal`,
     `src-mischel-shoda-1995-caps`.
   - Practice: name the story your body added before replying.

2. **The Apology Draft That Became a Dissertation**
   - Scene: "Sounds good" leads to seven rewritten paragraphs.
   - Concept: relatedness need, approval seeking, suppression.
   - Sources: `src-deci-ryan-2000-sdt`, `src-young-2003-schema-therapy`,
     `src-gross-john-2003-erq`.
   - Practice: send the repair, not the courtroom transcript.

3. **Your Spreadsheet Is Trying To Protect You**
   - Scene: planning becomes calming until it becomes a cage.
   - Concept: control-seeking coping, safety need, secondary appraisal.
   - Sources: `src-lazarus-folkman-1984-coping`, `src-scherer-2001-appraisal`.
   - Practice: separate useful control from ritualized control.

4. **The Disappearing Act After Conflict**
   - Scene: someone cares, then vanishes after the hard talk.
   - Concept: withdrawal, attachment avoidance, emotion regulation.
   - Sources: `src-fraley-2000-ecr-r`, `src-gross-1998-emotion-regulation`.
   - Practice: create a low-pressure re-entry script.

5. **When Advice Feels Like an Attack**
   - Scene: "Have you tried..." lands as "you are failing."
   - Concept: competence need, appraisal, self-protection.
   - Sources: `src-deci-ryan-2000-sdt`, `src-scherer-2001-appraisal`.
   - Practice: ask whether the person wants ideas, empathy, or backup.

6. **The Meeting Comment You Are Still Mad About**
   - Scene: tiny fairness violation, oversized mental replay.
   - Concept: fairness schema, moral appraisal, reappraisal.
   - Sources: `src-haidt-2012-moral-foundations`, `src-gross-1998-emotion-regulation`.
   - Practice: distinguish value signal from action demand.

7. **Why Choosing Dinner Can Become A Leadership Crisis**
   - Scene: low-stakes choices feel high-stakes when everyone is watching.
   - Concept: decision style, social influence, risk aversion.
   - Sources: `src-scott-bruce-1995-gdms`, DOSPERT/GDMS instrument graph.
   - Practice: decide what kind of decision this is before optimizing it.

8. **The Calm Face Is Not The Whole Weather Report**
   - Scene: someone looks composed while internally overloaded.
   - Concept: expressive suppression vs reappraisal.
   - Sources: `src-gross-1998-emotion-regulation`, `src-gross-john-2003-erq`.
   - Practice: check for cost, not just composure.

9. **Over-Functioning: A Love Language With Receipts**
   - Scene: being helpful becomes being responsible for everyone.
   - Concept: relatedness, esteem, self-sacrifice, people-pleasing.
   - Sources: `src-deci-ryan-2000-sdt`, `src-young-2003-schema-therapy`.
   - Practice: turn "I can do it" into "what is actually mine?"

10. **Burnout Is Not Just Being Tired**
    - Scene: rest does not restore because the system never stood down.
    - Concept: need frustration, recovery, coping load.
    - Sources: `src-deci-ryan-2000-sdt`, `src-lazarus-folkman-1984-coping`.
    - Practice: identify which need is still being frustrated.

11. **The Friend Who Wants Reassurance And The Friend Who Wants Space**
    - Scene: one person reaches, one person retreats.
    - Concept: attachment anxiety and avoidance as dimensions.
    - Sources: `src-fraley-2000-ecr-r`.
    - Practice: replace protest/withdrawal with a time-bound repair plan.

12. **What If We Could Rehearse The Hard Moment First?**
    - Scene: practicing a conversation before it happens.
    - Concept: simulation as reflection, provenance, bounded validity.
    - Sources: PsychSimulator README, run records, governance docs,
      `src-nist-ai-rmf`, `src-park-2023-generative-agents`.
    - Practice: use scenario rehearsal as preparation, not prophecy.

## Seasonal Arcs

Season 1: "Patterns You Can Recognize"

- Focus: therapy-adjacent concepts in ordinary life.
- Product mention: none or very light.
- Goal: trust, voice, community comments.

Season 2: "Patterns You Can Practice"

- Focus: scripts, tiny experiments, before/after reflections.
- Product mention: occasional "scenario rehearsal" framing.
- Goal: newsletter/community growth.

Season 3: "Patterns We Can Simulate Responsibly"

- Focus: what synthetic scenarios can teach, where they fail, why provenance and
  validation matter.
- Product mention: soft launch waitlist or pilot invitation if ready.
- Goal: simulator community and pilot feedback.

## Recurring Formats

- "Tiny script": one hard conversation in three safer versions.
- "What got activated?": same event, three possible internal meanings.
- "Not a diagnosis, a lens": public-safe concept explanation.
- "Before you reply": work communication reflection prompt.
- "The unsexy skill": small regulation practice that actually helps.

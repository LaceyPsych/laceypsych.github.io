# Blog 01 Architecture Under Pressure Spec

Date: 2026-07-15

Source issue: https://github.com/LaceyPsych/laceypsych.github.io/issues/2

## Goal

Implement the first public blog post, "A Person Is an Architecture Under
Pressure," as the opening essay for Lacey's Hugo/GitHub Pages site.

The post should make Human Architecture Theory readable without sounding like a
framework memo. It should feel like a simple story: a person receives a small
ordinary pressure, different inner parts wake up, and the reader learns that
behavior is generated by what was built, what is reachable, and what the road
allows.

## Public Shape

- Start with a concrete, low-stakes scene.
- Use child-simple language, short sentences, and light humor.
- Avoid diagnosis, treatment claims, simulator claims, or real/private
  identifying details.
- Name the core formula in public language:
  - what was built;
  - what was reachable;
  - what the road allowed.
- Close with a gentle reflection prompt and a plain educational boundary note.

## Simulator-Playable Narrative Shape

Keep this implicit in the public post.

Scenario frame:

- A neutral message arrives: "Can we talk about this tomorrow?"
- Contexts:
  - safe/repaired relationship;
  - judged relationship;
  - fatigue plus time pressure;
  - money or job uncertainty.
- Construct hooks:
  - `SCHEMA.trust`
  - `SCHEMA.worthiness`
  - `SCHEMA.safety`
  - `NEED.relatedness`
  - `NEED.autonomy`
  - `NEED.safety`
  - `COPE.people_pleasing`
  - `COPE.withdrawal`
  - `COPE.control_seeking`
  - `DECIDE.risk_averse`
  - `EMO.reactivity`
  - `EMO.recovery_speed`

Narrative characters should be fictional composites, not profile names. The
public post can show "same sentence, different meaning" without saying
"engine," "simulator," or "profile."

## Social Variants

LinkedIn:

```text
Sometimes the whole inner town wakes up because one message says, "Can we talk tomorrow?"

The useful question is not only "Why did I react like that?"

It is also:

What did this situation activate?
What was reachable under pressure?
What did the road actually allow?

Behavior matters. Impact matters. Accountability matters.

But people are not fixed traits moving through neutral rooms. We are layered systems inside bodies, histories, relationships, and constraints.

That lens can make us less blaming and more precise.
```

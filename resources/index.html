Resources

Reader-safe sources and starting points for the blog’s recurring themes.

These are not homework assignments. They are public doors into the ideas behind the essays.

Understanding Stress And Access

  • Stress signalling and prefrontal function, Amy Arnsten. A research anchor for why pressure can narrow working memory, flexible thinking, and reflective control.
  • Stress, adaptation, and brain plasticity, Bruce McEwen. A source for thinking about repeated stress, allostasis, and how experience can reshape future responses.

Context, Personality, And Patterns

  • A Cognitive-Affective System Theory of Personality, Walter Mischel and Yuichi Shoda. A foundation for understanding stable “if-then” patterns across different situations.
  • The Ecology of Human Development, Urie Bronfenbrenner. A clear reminder that people develop inside families, institutions, cultures, and historical worlds.

Constraint, Scarcity, And Choice

  • Scarcity, Sendhil Mullainathan and Eldar Shafir. A reader-friendly source for how money, time, and other limits can tax attention and narrow choices.

Resilience And Protective Factors

  • Ordinary Magic, Ann Masten. A grounding source for resilience as ordinary protective systems, not a rare heroic trait.
